The 30-Minute Hourglass Session

Each day, the Scribe dedicates one hourglass turn to handwritten study. This is not meditation in stillness, but meditation through action‚Äîthe moving meditation of ink on paper. Like a monk in a scriptorium, you preserve knowledge by writing it.

Session Protocol:

Before Beginning:

  • Brew your Study Session Tea
  • Light Ma'at's white quill (if using candles)
  • Open your grimoire notebook
  • Turn the hourglass

During the Session:

  • Write continuously until sand runs out
  • Document: insights, philosophies, learnings from the day
  • No digital devices‚Äîonly paper and pen
  • Let thoughts flow through hand to page

After Completion:

  • Close your notebook with gratitude
  • Extinguish candle if used
  • Place pen back in studiolo
This practice serves dual purposes: it preserves your wisdom in permanent form (honoring Thoth) while providing the contemplative breathing space your mind requires.

Regular Observances

These are the small workings that accumulate into a life of practice:

Charging Talismans

Hold object whilst concentrating intention. The focused will imprints upon matter. Do this whenever you feel your talismans need renewal.

Inscribing Symbols

Egyptian hieroglyphs, alchemical symbols, or sacred geometry. The act of writing channels Thoth's influence. Practice in margins of your grimoire or on separate parchment.

Arranging Shrine Objects

Placement communicates with divine forces. Intentional arrangement equals prayer without words. Adjust your studiolo when its energy feels stagnant.

Stirring Deosil

Clockwise stirring while holding intent. Whether tea, potions, or cooking‚Äîthe motion matters. Deosil (sunwise) for manifestation and growth.
