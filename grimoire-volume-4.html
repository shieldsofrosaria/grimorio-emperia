  • Setting new intentions
  • Beginning manifestation bottles
  • Planning future workings
  • Introspection and inner work
  • Divination for upcoming cycle

Best For:
  • Taking action on new moon intentions
  • Attracting abundance and opportunities
  • Building habits and routines
  • Creative projects gaining momentum
  • Charging manifestation bottles
"Mi kreskas kun la luno" (I grow with the moon)

  • Making important decisions
  • Overcoming obstacles and resistance
  • Using Caerus's Yes/No coin for guidance
  • Strength and courage workings

  • Refining and improving ongoing projects
  • Fine-tuning manifestations
  • Preparation for full moon workings
  • Building momentum toward completion
  • Patience and perseverance

  • Breaking bad habits
  • Ending unhealthy situations

  • Forgiveness and letting go

üåç Seasonal Markers

The Wheel of the Year turns through eight major points: solstices, equinoxes, and cross-quarter days. Your practice doesn't require celebrating all of them‚Äîobserve what resonates. The four solar points (solstices and equinoxes) are the foundation.

üå∏ Spring Equinox
~March 20-21 ‚Ä¢ Equal day and night, light increasing

Balance point tilting toward light. Seeds germinate, life returns, potential becomes visible. This is the year's new beginning.

Workings: New projects, planting intentions (literal or metaphorical), cleansing and renewal, fertility workings

"La vivo revenas, kaj mi kreskas" (Life returns, and I grow)
‚òÄÔ∏è Summer Solstice
~June 20-21 ‚Ä¢ Longest day, peak of light

Maximum solar power. Abundance, fullness, peak energy. Everything is in full bloom. Celebrate success and vitality.

Workings: Success magic, abundance, strength, charging solar talismans, outdoor sessions, fire workings

"La suno estas plena, kaj mi estas forta" (The sun is full, and I am strong)
üçÇ Autumn Equinox
~September 22-23 ‚Ä¢ Equal day and night, darkness increasing

Harvest time, gathering what was sown. Balance again, but tilting toward introspection. Gratitude for abundance, preparation for rest.

Workings: Gratitude, completion, preserving what matters, preparation, balance workings

"Mi rikoltas kun dankemo" (I harvest with gratitude)
‚ùÑÔ∏è Winter Solstice
~December 21-22 ‚Ä¢ Longest night, rebirth of light

The dark turns back toward light. In deepest darkness, hope returns. Reflection, renewal, honoring both death and rebirth.

Workings: Introspection, planning, honoring endings, welcoming new beginnings, ancestor work, hope and rebirth

"En la plej malhela nokto, lumo renaskiƒùas" (In the darkest night, light is reborn)
Additional Observation Points: The cross-quarter days (approximately February 1, May 1, August 1, and November 1) fall midway between solstices and equinoxes. These can be observed as natural transition markers if you feel called to them.
